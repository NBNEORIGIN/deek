# Signage and Menus Order Guide

This article covers the standard signage and menu ordering process for NBNE venues, based on a typical multi-location signage setup including roadside positioning, boundary markers, and printed menu materials. Use this as a template when coordinating similar orders with suppliers.

## Order Summary

When placing a comprehensive signage and menu order, break the request into clear categories: roadside signage, boundary/perimeter signage, promotional flags, and printed materials. Always confirm artwork requirements upfront to avoid delays.

## Signage Categories and Quantities

### 1. Roadside A-Frame Signs (Quantity: 3)

These are primary wayfinding tools positioned at strategic roadside locations:

- **Purpose**: Direct customers to venue entrance
- **Required features**: Directional arrows and QR codes
- **Positioning**: Space these at decision points along approach routes
- **Material consideration**: Must be weather-resistant and stable in wind

**Common pitfall**: Ensure QR codes are tested and link to the correct destination (menu, booking page, or venue information) before printing. Update any changed URLs in your content management system first.

### 2. Promotional Flags (Quantity: 4)

- **Purpose**: High-visibility markers, typically feather or teardrop style
- **Placement**: Entrance areas, car parks, or event spaces
- **Branding**: Confirm current brand guidelines are applied
- **Hardware**: Verify if bases/stakes are included or need separate ordering

### 3. Lane Entry Sign (Quantity: 1)

Described as "road sign style" for bottom of lane:

- **Style**: Professional directional signage mimicking highway standard signs
- **Mounting**: Typically post-mounted; confirm installation method with supplier
- **Visibility**: Should be readable from vehicle at approach speed
- **Regulations**: Check local signage regulations for size and placement restrictions

### 4. Boundary Line Sign (Quantity: 1)

- **Purpose**: Property demarcation or informational
- **Consider**: Whether this needs to communicate rules, welcome messages, or boundaries
- **Durability**: Likely requires robust outdoor materials

### 5. Walk Fence Tall Signs (Quantity: 2)

- **Height**: "Tall" suggests elevated visibility markers
- **Mounting**: Confirm fence attachment method won't damage existing structure
- **Purpose**: Likely wayfinding or branding along pedestrian routes

## Menu Printing Specifications

### Size and Material

- **Size**: A5 (148mm × 210mm) - confirmed as half A4
- **Material**: Upgrade from standard paper to **card stock** or **heavy weight paper** (minimum 250gsm recommended, 300gsm preferred for durability)
- **Finish options**: Consider matt or gloss lamination for spill resistance and longevity

### Quantity Considerations

The email requests "some more menus" without specifying quantity. Before ordering:

- Review current stock levels
- Calculate typical usage rate (how many per week/month)
- Order sufficient quantity to justify setup costs while avoiding obsolescence if menu changes seasonally
- **Recommended**: Order in batches of 100-250 for cost efficiency

**Warning**: Always proof menu content carefully before final print run. Check:
- Current prices
- Seasonal items availability
- Allergen information compliance
- Contact details and opening hours
- Spelling and formatting

## Artwork Requirements Checklist

Before confirming the order, verify with the supplier:

- [ ] Do they have current logo files and brand assets on record?
- [ ] Are new artwork files needed for any items?
- [ ] What file formats are required? (typically vector PDF, AI, or EPS for signage)
- [ ] What resolution is required for QR codes?
- [ ] Are there template dimensions available?
- [ ] Who is responsible for generating QR codes - client or supplier?

**Best practice**: Create a shared folder with approved, print-ready artwork files that suppliers can access for all orders.

## Order Process

1. **Consolidate requirements** - Compile all signage needs into one specification document
2. **Request itemized quote** - Get per-item pricing for budget approval
3. **Confirm artwork** - Resolve any graphic design needs before placing order
4. **Approve proofs** - Review digital proofs for every item, especially QR code functionality
5. **Schedule delivery** - Coordinate with installation timeline if applicable
6. **Plan installation** - Determine if signage requires professional installation or can be self-installed

## Cost Management

When requesting pricing:
- Ask for itemized breakdown (design, printing, hardware, delivery)
- Confirm if VAT is included
- Check delivery timeframes
- Inquire about bulk order discounts
- Consider establishing a regular supplier relationship for consistent pricing
