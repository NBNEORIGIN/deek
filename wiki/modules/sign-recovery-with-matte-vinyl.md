# Sign Recovery with Matte Vinyl

## Summary
Sign recovery involves applying a vinyl overlay to an existing sign substrate to refresh its appearance without replacing the entire sign. This article covers the process for recovering a sign using matte vinyl finish, which provides a non-reflective, premium appearance suitable for traditional pub and retail signage like the Hairy Lemon project.

## Materials Required
- Matte vinyl (appropriate grade for substrate and environment)
- Application fluid/slip solution
- Squeegee (felt-edged recommended for matte finishes)
- Cleaning solution (IPA or approved cleaner)
- Lint-free cloths
- Scalpel/trimming knife with fresh blades
- Masking tape (for alignment)

## Pre-Recovery Assessment

### 1. Inspect Existing Sign Substrate
- Check for structural integrity - no warping, delamination, or rot
- Verify substrate is suitable for vinyl application (aluminum, ACM, smooth timber)
- Examine surface condition - must be smooth and free from deep scratches
- Measure dimensions accurately including any returns or edges

### 2. Surface Preparation Requirements
Matte vinyl shows imperfections more readily than gloss finishes, so preparation is critical:
- Remove old vinyl completely if present
- Sand rough areas with 220-grit sandpaper
- Fill any dents or imperfections with appropriate filler
- Clean thoroughly with IPA to remove oils, dust, and residue
- Allow surface to dry completely (minimum 30 minutes)

⚠️ **WARNING**: Matte vinyl will telegraph any surface defects. Budget additional prep time compared to standard gloss applications.

## Application Process

### 3. Vinyl Sizing and Preparation
- Cut vinyl 50mm oversize on all edges for wrap-around or trimming
- Allow vinyl to acclimatize to workshop temperature (18-22°C) for minimum 2 hours
- For multi-panel designs, dry-fit all pieces before application

### 4. Application Technique
1. Position sign horizontally on clean application table
2. Mark alignment points with masking tape
3. For wet application method (recommended for matte):
   - Apply light, even coat of application fluid to substrate
   - Position vinyl using hinge method for larger signs
   - Work from centre outward with overlapping squeegee strokes
4. For dry application (smaller signs only):
   - Use low-tack application tape
   - Apply with firm, consistent pressure

### 5. Finishing
- Trim excess vinyl with sharp blade, holding at 45° angle
- Heat-seal edges if sign has exposed perimeter (use heat gun at medium setting)
- For wrapped edges, apply heat and pressure to ensure adhesion around corners
- Final squeegee pass with firm pressure
- Allow to cure 24 hours before installation

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **Matte Finish Considerations**:
- **Fingerprints**: Matte surfaces show fingerprints easily - wear clean cotton gloves during final handling
- **Squeegee marks**: Use lighter pressure than with gloss vinyl; matte shows streaking more readily
- **Cleaning**: Matte vinyl cannot be aggressively cleaned - inform customer of maintenance limitations
- **Adhesion test**: Always test a small section first if substrate is unusual

⚠️ **Environmental Factors**:
- Avoid application in temperatures below 15°C or above 25°C
- Humidity should be below 70% for optimal adhesion
- Don't apply in direct sunlight or on heat-saturated surfaces

## Quality Checks

Before releasing to installation:
- No bubbles or silvering visible
- All edges sealed (especially on external signs)
- Surface free from squeegee marks or contamination
- Colors match approved proof/sample
- Alignment correct on multi-panel signs

## Installation Considerations

Once recovered, the sign can be:
- Reinstalled to existing mounting points (verify weight hasn't changed significantly)
- Installed as new if this is the first mounting
- Coordinate with client regarding access, timings, and site requirements
