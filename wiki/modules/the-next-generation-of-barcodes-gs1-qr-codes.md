# The Next Generation of Barcodes (GS1 QR Codes)

GS1 UK is rolling out QR code capability within My Numberbank as part of their "Quiet Revolution" initiative. These GS1-powered QR codes represent the next generation of product identification, enabling brands to connect products to digital experiences, share trusted product data, and meet evolving retailer and regulatory requirements. This is more than traditional QR codes—it's a fundamental shift in how products are identified and experienced across the supply chain.

## Overview

Traditional 1D barcodes (the black and white lines we're all familiar with) are being supplemented—and eventually replaced—by 2D codes, specifically GS1-compliant QR codes. These new codes can store significantly more data and enable direct consumer engagement when scanned with smartphones.

**Key differences:**
- **Traditional barcodes**: Store basic product identification (GTIN)
- **GS1 QR codes**: Store GTIN plus additional data including batch numbers, expiry dates, serial numbers, and URLs to digital content

## Why This Matters for NBNE Operators

### 1. Retailer Requirements
Major retailers are beginning to mandate 2D barcode capability. Some retailers already require or strongly prefer products with scannable QR codes that link to detailed product information.

### 2. Regulatory Compliance
Certain product categories (particularly food, pharmaceuticals, and regulated goods) are facing increasing requirements for traceability data that traditional barcodes cannot accommodate.

### 3. Consumer Engagement
QR codes allow customers to access:
- Detailed product information
- Allergen and nutritional data
- Sustainability credentials
- Usage instructions and recipes
- Authenticity verification

### 4. Supply Chain Visibility
Enhanced data capture throughout the supply chain enables better tracking, recall management, and inventory control.

## Upcoming My Numberbank Capability

GS1 UK is introducing tools within My Numberbank to generate and manage these next-generation barcodes. This will allow NBNE members to:

- Create GS1-compliant QR codes for products
- Link product GTINs to digital content
- Manage and update product information centrally
- Future-proof packaging designs

## Implementation Considerations

### Packaging Design

**⚠️ WARNING**: QR codes require more physical space than traditional barcodes and have specific print quality requirements. Plan packaging redesigns carefully.

- **Minimum size**: QR codes need adequate "quiet zone" (white space) around them
- **Print quality**: Higher resolution printing may be required
- **Placement**: Consider both retail scanning needs and consumer accessibility
- **Dual coding period**: You'll likely need both traditional barcodes AND QR codes during transition period

### Data Management

QR codes can link to dynamic content, meaning the destination data can be updated without changing the printed code. However:

- You must maintain the digital infrastructure (or use GS1's services)
- Product data must be accurate and kept current
- Different markets may require different information
- Consider data hosting requirements and costs

### Technical Requirements

1. **URL structure**: GS1 Digital Link standard must be followed
2. **Domain ownership**: Ensure you control the domain used in QR codes
3. **Redirect management**: Plan for long-term URL maintenance
4. **Mobile optimization**: Linked content must work on smartphones

## Common Pitfalls

### Pitfall 1: Generating Non-Compliant QR Codes
Not all QR codes are GS1-compliant. Using standard QR generators rather than GS1 tools can create codes that won't work with retail scanning systems.

### Pitfall 2: Broken Links
Printing QR codes that link to URLs you don't control or that may change creates customer experience issues and compliance problems.

### Pitfall 3: Ignoring Retail Scanning
QR codes must still scan reliably at retail checkouts. Test with actual retail scanning equipment, not just smartphones.

### Pitfall 4: Inadequate Print Quality
Poor printing, wrong colors, or damaged codes will fail to scan. QR codes are more sensitive to print quality issues than traditional barcodes.

### Pitfall 5: Inconsistent Data
If your QR code links to product information that contradicts on-pack claims or other databases, it creates compliance and trust issues.
