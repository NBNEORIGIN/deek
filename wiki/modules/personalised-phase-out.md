# NBNE Personalised Product Phase-Out Strategy

Compiled April 2026. For Cairn memory and ongoing strategic reference.

## The Problem

Personalised products are structurally unprofitable at scale. Labour scales 1:1 with orders (every order requires human file setup and one-off production), while generic products scale approximately 1:5 via batch manufacturing, template design, and n8n automation. At the main personalised memorial SKU price of 19.99, after Amazon fees and materials roughly 10-11 remains to cover personalisation labour.

The same staff (Sanna) who produces personalised products also publishes generic products. Every hour on personalised is a direct opportunity cost against generic catalogue growth. Personalised products cannot be FBA'd at volume.

## Strategic Decision

Phase out all personalised products and replace with generic products over 18-24 months. Not because personalised products are unprofitable in isolation, but because they prevent scaling, consume disproportionate staff time, and generic products are a better long-term business.

## Three-Phase Plan

### Phase 1: Black and White Memorial Range (DONE)

MUTOH printer degraded from 12 units/hour to 2-3, making black acrylic memorial stakes loss-making. Decision: turn off entirely. Metallic/coloured variants on functioning equipment capture diverted demand. Sanna's time released to generic publishing.

### Phase 2: Main Personalised Memorial Listing (Price Ladder)

Primary product ASIN B07J3HDB2Y / B0CVHJBB5Z. 311 reviews, 4.8 stars, Amazon's Choice, 19.99. Strategy: price-led phase-out rather than hard switch-off.

Price ladder schedule:
- Phase 1 (now to end May 2026): 19.99 to 22.99 (OVERDUE)
- Phase 2 (June-August 2026): 22.99 to 25.99
- Phase 3 (September-November 2026): 25.99 to 29.99
- Phase 4 (January 2027): evaluate maintain or wind down

In parallel: push pre-designed generic memorial SKUs (Dad, Mum, Brother, Baby, pets) at or below 19.99 on Prime FBA. Amazon algorithm redirects price-sensitive buyers from personalised to generics automatically.

### Phase 3: Broader Personalised Catalogue (18-24 months)

All remaining personalised SKUs. Mathematical reality: 6-month product maturity period on Amazon, 7-8% success rate on generics, roughly 12-15 new products per month sustained over 18-24 months replaces the personalised revenue base.

Recommended launch rate: 25-30 products per month (target 18-month). Current publish rate since December 2025: approximately 256 products per month (verify sustainable rate with Sanna/Gabby).

## Generic Product Strategy

Pre-designed relationship-specific memorial SKUs feel personal to buyers but require zero personalisation labour. Batch-produced, FBA-shipped, Prime-eligible.

Key categories to expand: push/pull sign variants, safety signs, toilet/bathroom signs, memorial pre-designed range, French language variants (growing FR marketplace), large format signs (STALIN blank underexploited).

Success metrics tracked: success rate per published product (target over 10%, currently 7-8%), time to first sale (benchmark under 90 days), average successful product monthly revenue (target 400+/month at maturity).

## Financial Context

Personalised products estimated at approximately 13k/month revenue (revised from initial estimates after live data analysis). At 7-8% success rate and 400/month per mature product: need approximately 33-34 successfully matured generic products to replace. Revenue gap during transition manageable if generic publishing rate is maintained.
