# Sign Survey Site Visits — NBNE Measurement and Assessment Practice

## Overview

Site surveys are essential for accurate quoting, identifying access requirements, planning permissions issues, and spotting opportunities to add value. This article consolidates NBNE's internal practices for conducting sign surveys based on real project work.

## Pre-Survey Coordination

### Scheduling
- Coordinate directly with client to ensure site access and decision-maker presence
- Typical lead time: within 1 week of initial enquiry
- Standard windows offered: "lunchtime onwards" or specific AM/PM slots (e.g. "Thursday 18 September (AM) or Friday 19 September (PM)")
- For closed/empty sites, confirm whether client presence is required or if independent survey is acceptable

## What to Measure and Record

### Dimensions Required
Record all relevant measurements in a format designers can use immediately. From the dentist project (October 2024), good practice includes:

**Door/window openings:**
- Height and width (e.g. "Front door - 1.76m high and 0.69m wide")
- Adjacent features that may accommodate signage

**Wall areas for standoff signs:**
- Overall available width and height
- Obstructions (lights, heat pumps, handrails) with their positions
- Realistic working area (e.g. "2m squared area as he doesn't want it to go above the light")
- Depth of space (if applicable, though "not essential as image is just on the back wall")

**Room/area specifics:**
- Full dimensions: width, height, depth where relevant
- Heights to fixed features (e.g. "2.15m high to the heat pump")
- Width measurements to obstructions
- Themed requirements (e.g. "Children's area: 3.1m wide and 2.65m high. This is to be green themed, potential jungle book design")

### Sign Size Approximations
When clients lack tape measures, suggest standard sizes:
- Paper/window signs: 12" × 8" is a common recommendation
- Match existing signage dimensions where replacing like-for-like
- Projecting signs: "Most customers tend to go with a 600mm square size"

### Projecting Sign Specifications
For custom projecting signs (September 2025 drawing):
- Standard size: 600mm square
- Material: 30mm box section frame
- **Customisable standoff distance** (called "variable" in drawings) — critical for clearing wooden fascias
- Example project: 210mm standoff distance
- Mounting: bolt frame onto wall using anchors

## Value-Add Observations During Survey

### Planning Permission Opportunities
When existing signs are present: "This will also remove any issue with planning permission as it will be just upgrading a sign that already exists" — recommend like-for-like replacement to avoid planning complications.

### Visibility Assessment
Proactively identify visibility issues: "Currently, many of the signs are either partially or fully obscured until you enter the site." 

**Suggest relocations** with photo mark-ups (yellow highlighting) showing high-visibility alternatives. Frame as constructive: "Please forgive me if this comes across as overly forward—my intention is only to highlight this now so you have all the options on the table before committing to a direction."

### Cost-Effective Alternatives
For large/complex existing signage: "A replacement/relocation might prove more cost-effective than updating, given the scope of work required."

## Access and Ground Conditions (External Signs)

For larger external installations, survey must check:
- Access for equipment (tower, mini-digger if foundation work required)
- Ground conditions for tower footing
- Wall fixing points
- Existing foundation adequacy (see Safety note below)

## Material Recommendations Based on Site Conditions

### External Sign Longevity by Specification
For **remote, south-west facing, elevated** locations (high UV/wind exposure):

**3mm ACM with graphics:**
- Service life: ~5–7 years before refresh needed
- ACM substrate lasts longer; face graphics fade first
- Graphics: laminated print or cut vinyl

**Powder-coated aluminium + cut cast vinyl:**
- Panel: ~12–15 years before repaint/replacement
- Vinyl lettering: ~7–10 years (can be re-lettered while panel remains sound)

**Recommendation:** "If you want the longest service interval with the fewest future changes (and/or the ability to update/change the design), I recommend powder-coated aluminium + cut white cast vinyl."

## Internal Signs — Special Surfaces

### Bamboo/Non-Standard Walls
For surfaces like bamboo where drilling is undesirable:
- Option: flush-mount clear acrylic panel using high-bond tape with primer
- **Test adhesion during survey** and confirm longevity expectations
- If tape unsuitable, suggest lightweight alternatives (printed panel, slim free-standing frame)

## Safety and Structural Considerations

**WARNING:** For large roadside signs (≥2m × 2m near major roads), structural engineering is mandatory. A September 2025 incident identified:
- Inadequate post depth and foundation design
- Wind-induced movement and fatigue failures
- Missing structural calculations to EN 1991-1-4

**Required before work proceeds:**
- Structural design package signed by chartered engineer
- As-built details and fixing schedule
- Foundation design verification
- Project-specific RAMS

Flag any observed structural inadequacy immediately and STOP WORK.

## Post-Survey Actions

1. Confirm exact dimensions and material choice with client
2. Send digital proofs
3. Issue pro-forma invoice for 25% deposit
4. Communicate lead times (typically 7–10 working days from proof sign-off; 10–15 if powder-coating involved)
5. Schedule installation (can bundle internal signs with external visit)

Capture measurements in immediately usable format for designers. Good example structure from dentist project shows room-by-room breakdown with all constraints and client preferences noted inline.
