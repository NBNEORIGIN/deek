## Order Process

### 1. Purchase Orders Required

Elite Powder Coatings **requires a Purchase Order (PO) before commencing work**. This requirement was explicitly requested on 14/11/2024 and reiterated when delays occurred. Failure to issue a PO will result in work not starting, even after quotes are accepted and parts are ready for delivery.

**Critical lesson**: Always issue PO immediately after quote acceptance to avoid project delays.

### 2. Payment Methods

Elite Powder Coatings accepts:
- BACS transfer (via proforma invoice)
- Card payment on collection

Both methods confirmed as acceptable by Emma Henderson (Office Manager) on 29/11/2024.

### 3. Typical Lead Times

Based on November 2024 correspondence:
- Standard turnaround appears to be approximately 2 weeks from PO issuance
- Rush jobs can be accommodated (one instance showed completion target of Monday for Friday request on 22/11/2024)
- Always confirm lead times at quote stage, particularly for time-sensitive projects

## Part Preparation for Powder Coating

### Hanging Considerations

**Critical learning from November 2024 project**: Not all parts can be hung in the powder coating oven using standard methods.

**Issue identified**: Small or awkwardly shaped letters presented hanging challenges for Elite Powder Coatings.

**Solution proposed by supplier**: Stick difficult-to-hang letters to a backing plate for coating process.

**Design implication**: When specifying whether part backs are critical:
- Parts facing walls or hidden from view: backs are **not critical** and can be mounted to plates
- Parts visible from multiple angles: backs **are critical** and alternative preparation methods must be discussed at quote stage

### Pre-delivery Communication

Before delivering parts to Elite Powder Coatings:
1. Confirm drop-off arrangements
2. Verify payment method preference
3. Ensure PO has been issued
4. Discuss any unusual part geometries or mounting requirements
5. Confirm lead time expectations

### Checklist for Powder Coating Orders

Before contacting Elite Powder Coatings:

1. **Parts ready**: Ensure all items are fabricated and ready for coating
2. **Quote obtained**: Request written quote with specific colour/finish specifications
3. **PO raised**: Issue PO immediately upon quote acceptance
4. **Delivery arranged**: Confirm drop-off date/time with supplier
5. **Payment method confirmed**: Specify BACS or card payment preference
6. **Collection date agreed**: Confirm completion timeline and collection arrangements

### During Production

- Elite will typically message when parts are ready for collection
- For unusual parts (small letters, complex geometries), expect mid-project communication about hanging/mounting solutions
- Provide clear guidance on which surfaces are critical vs. can be compromised for process requirements

## Technical Considerations

### Part Geometry Issues

**Small letters and signage components** may present challenges:
- Cannot always be hung using standard hooks/wires
- May require mounting to backing plates for coating process
- Discuss mounting approach at quote stage for complex geometries

### Critical vs. Non-Critical Surfaces

When commissioning powder coating, always specify:
- **Critical surfaces**: Those that will be visible in final installation (e.g., front faces of wall-mounted letters)
- **Non-critical surfaces**: Those that will be hidden (e.g., backs of wall-mounted letters)

This specification allows the supplier to use backing plates or alternative mounting methods without compromising finish quality on visible surfaces.
