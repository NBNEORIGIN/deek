# Colour Matching and Pantone Vinyl — NBNE Supplier Guide and Material Selection

## Overview

Matching specific colours for vinyl graphics, particularly when clients request Pantone references or custom colours, requires understanding material options, supplier capabilities, and the practical limitations of vinyl vs printed alternatives. This article consolidates NBNE's supplier relationships and material choices for colour-critical jobs.

## Primary Vinyl Supplier: FFIS

**FFIS** (contact: Lyndsey Gray - lgray@ffis.co.uk, Amanda Vasey - avasey@ffis.co.uk) is NBNE's primary vinyl supplier for both standard and specialty colour requirements.

### Standard Colour Vinyl Orders

For vehicle graphics and general signage, NBNE typically orders:
- **Metamark MD5** or similar grade cast vinyl for vehicle applications
- Standard roll width: **610mm**
- Minimum order quantities: As low as 5m accepted for standard colours

**Example order (April 2026):** Red vinyl for vehicle decals - 610mm roll, 5m length, Metamark MD5 grade. FFIS enquired whether "post box red" would be acceptable, indicating they stock common colour references but may need specification for exact Pantone matches.

### Specialty Material Enquiries

When NBNE requires advice on specific applications, FFIS provides material recommendations:
- **Air release vinyl** for large-format installations
- Application spray recommendations for challenging installs
- Material suitability guidance (internal vs external, substrate compatibility)

**Example (November 2024):** For a large sign installation (~5m x 1.2m) on ACM panels, NBNE consulted FFIS about air release vinyl and application spray to ensure proper alignment of tiled graphics.

## Colour Matching Limitations: Vinyl vs Printing

### When Vinyl Colour Matching Fails

**Case Study (February 2026):** Client requested brown 3D lettering to match existing signage. The specified brown acrylic was:
- Not available from UK suppliers
- Required sourcing from **Belgium**
- Caused significant project delays

**Alternative solution offered:** UV printed and laminated vinyl as a faster alternative to waiting for specialty acrylic. This suggests printed solutions offer broader colour matching capabilities than relying on manufacturer-stocked vinyl colours.

### Pantone Matching Considerations

The emails don't contain specific examples of Pantone-matched vinyl orders, but the red vinyl enquiry pattern suggests:
- Standard colour descriptors ("post box red") are more practical than Pantone references when ordering pre-manufactured vinyl
- Custom Pantone matches may require printed vinyl rather than manufactured colour vinyl
- Image Perfect brand mentioned as acceptable for standard red applications

## UV Roll-to-Roll Printing for Colour Matching

NBNE owns UV roll-to-roll printing equipment, offering superior colour flexibility:

### Advantages Over Manufactured Vinyl
- **Any colour achievable** through CMYK printing process
- Can match brown lettering and custom colours exactly (as offered in February 2026 case)
- Suitable for large-format tiling applications
- Allows for printed graphics where vinyl colour selection is limited

### Material Specifications
- Printable vinyl for UV roll-to-roll system
- Requires lamination for durability
- Can be applied to ACM panels and other substrates
- Suitable for vertical "wallpaper style" installation in strips

## Material Grade Selection by Application

### Vehicle Graphics
- **Metamark MD5** or equivalent cast vinyl
- Premium grade required for durability and conformability
- Colour availability: Standard manufacturer palette

### Large Format Signage
- UV printed vinyl preferred for colour accuracy
- Air release vinyl recommended for installations over 1.2m width
- Application spray used for repositioning during alignment

### 3D Letters and Dimensional Signage
- Acrylic in custom colours (sourced internationally if required)
- UV printed vinyl as faster alternative when colour-matched acrylic unavailable
- Black acrylic suggested as alternative when matching existing elements

## Best Practices for Colour-Critical Projects

1. **Assess colour matching method early:** Determine if manufactured vinyl colours will suffice or if printing is required
2. **Consult FFIS before quoting:** Verify colour availability and material suitability
3. **Consider UV printing alternative:** When exact Pantone match required or specialty colours needed
4. **Plan for international sourcing delays:** Non-standard acrylic colours may require 2+ weeks from Belgium
5. **Test samples before installation:** As demonstrated by rejected Gateshead cream panel - colour differences are noticeable on large installations
6. **Offer printed alternatives:** When material lead times conflict with client deadlines
