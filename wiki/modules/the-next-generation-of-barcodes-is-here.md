# The Next Generation of Barcodes is Here

GS1 UK is introducing QR code capabilities powered by GS1 standards as the next generation of product identification. This "Quiet Revolution" enables products to connect to rich digital experiences, trusted data, and enhanced consumer engagement. The new capability will soon be available within My Numberbank, allowing NBNE operators to future-proof packaging, meet retailer and regulatory expectations, and unlock additional value from every product scan.

## What's Changing

Traditional linear barcodes (the black and white stripes we're all familiar with) are being supplemented—and in some cases replaced—by 2D barcodes, specifically QR codes that comply with GS1 standards.

### Key Differences

1. **Data capacity**: QR codes can store significantly more information than traditional barcodes
2. **Functionality**: Each scan can connect to dynamic, updateable digital content
3. **Consumer engagement**: Customers can scan products with their smartphones to access product information, provenance data, allergen warnings, and more
4. **Regulatory compliance**: Meets growing requirements for product traceability and information disclosure

## Why This Matters for NBNE

1. **Future-proofing**: Major retailers are moving toward requiring 2D barcodes
2. **Enhanced traceability**: Better lot/batch tracking capabilities
3. **Customer experience**: Direct engagement opportunities with end consumers
4. **Regulatory readiness**: Compliance with upcoming regulations requiring enhanced product information
5. **Supply chain visibility**: Improved data sharing across the supply chain

## Implementation Through My Numberbank

GS1 UK is introducing new capabilities within the My Numberbank platform to support this transition:

1. **Product connection**: Ability to link products to digital experiences
2. **Data management**: Centralized control of product information
3. **QR code generation**: Tools to create GS1-compliant QR codes
4. **Information hosting**: Platform to share trusted product data

### Expected Timeline

The capability is described as "coming very soon" to My Numberbank. Watch for:

- Email notifications from GS1 UK
- Updates to the My Numberbank interface
- Training materials and guidance documentation

## Preparing Your Products

### Steps to Consider

1. **Audit current packaging**: Identify products with upcoming packaging redesigns or reprints
2. **Review data completeness**: Ensure product information is comprehensive and accurate in existing systems
3. **Prioritize high-value products**: Focus initially on products where enhanced consumer engagement offers the most benefit
4. **Coordinate with retailers**: Discuss requirements and timelines with key retail partners
5. **Plan packaging updates**: Work with packaging suppliers to incorporate QR codes into designs

### Information to Prepare

Have the following data ready for products:

- Complete product descriptions
- Allergen information
- Ingredient lists
- Country of origin
- Sustainability/provenance information
- Nutritional data
- Usage instructions
- Batch/lot information management processes

## ⚠️ Warnings and Common Pitfalls

**Don't rush packaging changes**: Ensure you understand retailer requirements before committing to new packaging designs. Requirements may vary by retailer and category.

**Data quality is critical**: Unlike traditional barcodes, QR codes can expose product data directly to consumers. Inaccurate or incomplete information creates brand risk and potential regulatory issues.

**Not a simple replacement**: This isn't just swapping one barcode for another. It requires infrastructure to host and manage digital product information.

**Maintain traditional barcodes**: For the foreseeable future, you'll likely need both traditional linear barcodes AND QR codes on packaging. Don't remove linear barcodes without explicit retailer approval.

**Test before rollout**: Verify QR codes scan correctly across different devices and scanning applications before committing to large packaging runs.

**Link management**: Dead links or outdated information damage brand credibility. Establish processes to maintain digital content over product lifecycles.
