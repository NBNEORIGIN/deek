# Sign Survey Site Visits — Measurement and Documentation Standards

## Overview

Site surveys are a critical stage in the quotation and production process at NBNE. A thorough survey minimises returns, reduces installation delays, and ensures designs are appropriate for the space. This article documents NBNE's internal practices for conducting sign surveys based on actual project emails.

## When to Conduct a Survey

Always offer a site survey before providing a formal quotation for:

- **Building-mounted external signage** (fascia signs, stand-off letters, illuminated trays)
- **Projecting signs** requiring custom standoff distances or bracket specifications
- **Internal wayfinding or branded signage** in complex spaces (reception areas, surgery rooms, stairwells)
- **Sign replacements** where existing fixing marks need covering or planning permission continuity matters
- **Any installation involving access equipment**, unusual substrates (bamboo, glass partitions), or proximity to infrastructure (heat pumps, lighting)

For straightforward jobs like paper posters or small window graphics, clients can self-measure (e.g. "12" x 8"" suggested for shop window notices), but always recommend a survey if the client expresses uncertainty about fit or positioning.

## Survey Coordination and Client Attendance

**Standard practice**: Offer the client the option to attend but do not require it unless they request input on design positioning.

- Example wording: *"We are very happy to attend the site and conduct a survey... Would you like to be on site for the survey to commence or is it fine if we attend site and feedback to you via email?"*
- If the client wants to attend, coordinate specific times (e.g. "Wednesday lunchtime onwards" or "Thursday AM / Friday PM").
- For closed or restricted sites, confirm whether the client or a keyholder will provide access.

## What to Measure and Document

### Dimensions
Record all relevant measurements in **metres** (not feet/inches) and specify:

- **Width, height, and depth** of installation areas
- **Obstructions**: lights, heat pumps, handrails, windows, doors
- **Working envelope**: Calculate usable space after accounting for obstructions (e.g. "2m squared area for this one as he doesn't want it to go above the light")
- **Existing signage dimensions** if replacement/upgrade is planned (critical for planning permission continuity)

### Example from Dentist Project:
- Reception stand-off design: 2.9m wide, 2.4m high, 1.9m deep (depth noted as "not essential as image is just on the back wall")
- Stairwell: Light is 30cm diameter; wall is 2.76m wide overall, 2.06m wide to the light; wall height from handrail is 3.5m; realistic working area is 2m² maximum
- Surgery rooms: 3.86m wide, 2.5m high overall, 2.15m high to heat pump, 1.6m to heat pump width

### Fixings and Access
- **Wall substrate**: brick, render, timber fascia, bamboo, glass partition, etc.
- **Fixing method**: direct drill, standoff brackets, high-bond tape with primer (for non-drillable surfaces)
- **Access requirements**: tower hire, scaffolding, exclusion zones (especially for roadside A1 installations)
- **Ground conditions** if post-mounted signs require foundations

### Projecting Signs
For projecting/blade signs, document:

- **Standoff distance** (variable; example: 210mm from wooden fascia to allow clearance)
- **Material**: typically 30mm box section
- **Most common customer choice**: 600mm square size

### Photos
Take photos during every survey to:

- Illustrate **obstruction issues** (signs obscured until entering site)
- Highlight **alternative positioning opportunities** (mark in yellow/annotation)
- Document **existing signage condition** (disbonded panels, corrosion, post movement)
- Support recommendations for relocation vs. replacement

## Post-Survey Communication

### Feedback to Client
After the survey, provide:

1. **Constructive observations**: If existing signs are poorly positioned or obscured, suggest relocations with annotated photos. Frame as cost-saving: *"I'd hate for you to incur unnecessary costs later if a small adjustment now could offer better long-term value."*
2. **Material recommendations**: Based on exposure (UV, wind, elevation). Example:
   - **ACM with graphics**: ~5–7 year refresh cycle (remote, south-west facing, elevated sites)
   - **Powder-coated aluminium + cut vinyl**: panel lasts ~12–15 years; vinyl ~7–10 years, can be re-lettered
3. **Optional alternatives**: Present original brief + cost-effective alternatives (e.g. replace roadside sign post entirely vs. update existing)

### Internal Documentation
- Store survey measurements in reusable format (PDF or shared doc)
- Example reference: `Survey_Razzle_Dazzle_Jewels_2025-11-29.pdf`
- For similar repeat jobs, create **design basis sheets** with revision control (e.g. "C173 - WINDOW SIGN - DESIGN BASIS - REV 3.0.jpg")

## Risk Assessment

A **standardised on-site risk assessment template** exists (Google Doc link shared internally 12/12/2024). 

### Key items to address:
- Hazardous substances (ensure text is updated to make sense per site)
- Working at height (ladder, tower, MEWP)
- Traffic management and exclusion zones (especially for A1-adjacent sites)
- Lifting/handling of large panels
- Wind hold points for high-exposure installations

**Process**: Add customer name to template, file for reference, and update site-specific hazards before each visit.

## Structural and Compliance Considerations

For **large roadside signs** (e.g. 2000×2000mm panels at 3–4m height beside A1):

⚠️ **Planning, insurance, and generic RAMS do not prove structural adequacy.**

Required before work proceeds:
- **Structural design package** (EN 1991-1-4, SLS/ULS) signed by chartered engineer
- **As-built details**: post sizes/grades/wall thickness, rail/fixing layout, foundation design
- **Project-specific RAMS**: traffic management, wind hold points, exclusion zones
- **Installer competence verification**

If survey reveals inadequate foundations (post movement, loose posts, shallow depth), **STOP WORK** and specify excavation/new reinforced concrete foundations before installation.

## Common Survey Scenarios

| Scenario | Key Measurements | Notes |
|----------|------------------|-------|
| Entrance/front door graphics | Door height/width + adjacent window dimensions | Check for vision panels, handle clearance |
| Stairwell signs | Wall dimensions, handrail height, lighting positions | Work around fixed lighting; calculate safe working envelope |
| Reception/internal branding | Wall width/height, desk sightlines, depth (if 3D) | Depth may be "not essential" if 2D wall-mount |
| Surgery/room ID signs | Door width, wall space to obstructions (heat pumps, etc.) | Recommend ~1/3 A4 size for door signs |
| Projecting blade signs | Fascia depth, required standoff, bracket fixing substrate | 600mm² is most popular; use 30mm box section |
| External replacement signs | Existing sign dimensions, fixing marks, substrate condition | Match size to avoid new planning permission |

## Approval and Next Steps

After survey and client material choice, send:
- Digital proofs (multiple colour/alignment options if applicable)
- Pro-forma invoice for **25% deposit**
- Lead time: **7–10 working days** from proof sign-off (10–15 days if powder-coating required)
- Schedule internal/external installs for same visit where possible to reduce mobilisation costs
