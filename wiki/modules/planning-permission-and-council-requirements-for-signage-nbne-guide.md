# Planning Permission and Council Requirements for Signage — NBNE Guide

## Overview

This article covers NBNE's practical experience with planning permission, advertisement consent, and council requirements when fabricating and installing signage, particularly in Alnwick town centre and Northumberland County.

## Key Authorities and Contacts

**Northumberland County Council — Planning Department**
- Email: planning@northumberland.gov.uk
- Address: County Hall, Morpeth, Northumberland
- Online services: www.northumberland.gov.uk (use 'pay, apply or report' portal)
- **Important**: Central Registry Team handles all enquiries via automated system; case officers do not correspond directly with third parties

**Separate departments to note:**
- **Planning Enforcement Team**: For reporting breaches or unauthorised works
- **Building Control**: Separate from planning permission; handles structural/safety compliance
- Private approved inspectors may also handle Building Control functions

## Alnwick Town Centre — Material Restrictions

**Critical requirement (as of October 2024):**

Northumberland Council is **currently refusing plans specifying plastics as the base material within Alnwick town centre**.

**NBNE standard response:**
- Specify **powder coated aluminium** as base material for all Alnwick centre applications
- Include "appropriate heavy duty fixings" in specifications
- Use this specification proactively in quotations and technical drawings submitted to clients for council approval

This restriction has been applied consistently across multiple projects (emails dated October–December 2024 with duncan@Northumbriadc.co.uk).

## Material Alternatives When Council Requires Non-Plastic

When councils insist on wood or metal components:

**Standard NBNE substitution:**
- Replace acrylic with **Aluminium Dibond**
- Maintains aesthetic while satisfying material requirements
- Applies to both fascia and stand-off signage

## Planning Application Support — Client-Led Process

**NBNE's role:**
- Clients typically submit their own planning applications
- NBNE supplies **supporting information** (drawings, specifications, material details) upon request
- We prepare drawings "for the basis of these [applications]" using client-supplied architectural drawings (e.g., Harper drawings referenced multiple times)

**Retrospective applications:**
- NBNE has offered to support retrospective planning applications when signage preferences change mid-project
- Example: When client preferred white logo requiring different background colour inconsistent with approved plans

## Advertisement Consent vs Planning Permission

**Advertisement consent** is a separate consideration, particularly for:
- Large private sign structures adjacent to highways (e.g., A1)
- Roadside/billboard-style signage
- Signage visible from major roads

Council automated response (Excerpt 1, September 2025) confirms the distinction between:
1. Planning permission (development/building works)
2. Advertisement consent (signage-specific)
3. Building regulations (structural safety — separate department)

## Structural/Safety Requirements for Large or Highway-Adjacent Signs

**Critical case study — A1 roadside signs (2025):**

NBNE supplied four 2000×2000 mm ACM sign faces (graphics only) to The Doxford Group for installation adjacent to the A1. **NBNE was not engaged to design or install supporting structure.**

**Safety concerns identified:**
- Panel-to-rail separation at southbound sign
- Uncertain/inadequate foundations (perceptible post movement)
- Eccentric mounting, improvised bracing, uncapped tube ends
- General deterioration contributing to potential fatigue or detachment

**NBNE issued formal STOP/MAKE-SAFE advisory requiring:**

1. **Structural design package** signed by suitably qualified/chartered engineer
2. Wind-load basis to **EN 1991-1-4 (UK National Annex)**
3. Specification for posts (diameter/grade/wall thickness), rails, fixing layout/spacing
4. **Foundation design** (dimensions/depth, concrete grade, reinforcement, ground assumptions)
5. **RAMS** (Risk Assessment & Method Statement) covering:
   - Lifting/handling
   - Working at height
   - Exclusion zones
   - Traffic management for highway-adjacent work
   - Weather/wind hold points
6. **Evidence of installer competence** (relevant cards/tickets)
7. **Public/products liability insurance**
8. **Necessary permissions/consents** including landowner consent and planning/advertisement consent if applicable

**NBNE position:**
- "We do not endorse proceeding without these controls"
- "We accept no liability for transport/handling/installation or in-service performance where we are not the installer"
- Matter was escalated to Professional Indemnity insurers as notification (no claim made)

**Lesson:** For large panels (2×2 m+) at height near high-speed roads, structural engineering certification and competent installation are non-negotiable safety requirements, regardless of who owns the installation contract.

## Temporary Signage as Planning Workaround

**Option during planning delays:**
- Install temporary, smaller sign (e.g., 4'×4') in correct brand colours
- NBNE can fabricate and install quickly
- Allows client to maintain presence while formal application processes
- Suggested in multiple October 2024 emails during tight deadline situations

## Timescales and Lead Times Affected by Planning

**Example from October 2024 project:**
- Sign trays (powder-coated metal construction) required manufacture and delivery lead time
- Could not be delivered before October 13 for installation to meet October 24 deadline
- **Planning approval delays compound manufacturing constraints**
- NBNE explored alternative suppliers for faster turnaround when planning-related delays compressed timelines

**Recommendation:** Factor planning approval timescales (typically weeks) into project schedules before committing to installation dates.

## Material Longevity and Resubmission Planning

Different materials have different outdoor lifespans, relevant when considering whether planning-approved signage will require future replacement/reapplication:

- **ACM with laminated graphics**: 5–7 years (graphics fade first; substrate lasts longer)
- **Powder-coated aluminium panel with cut vinyl**: Panel lasts 12–15 years; vinyl lettering 7–10 years (can be re-lettered while panel remains sound)

For exposed locations (UV/wind), powder-coated aluminium offers longest service interval and may reduce future planning resubmissions.

## NBNE Liability Position

**Clear boundaries established:**
- NBNE accepts liability for supplied goods (sign faces, graphics)
- When not engaged as installer, **no liability accepted** for installation, foundations, structural performance, or in-service safety
- Written disclaimers issued when safety concerns identified with third-party installation plans
- Professional Indemnity insurers notified of potential risks as precautionary measure
