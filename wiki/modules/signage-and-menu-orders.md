# Signage and Menu Orders

This article covers the standard process for ordering external signage (A-frames, flags, boundary markers, and fence signs) and printed menus for NBNE venues. Based on a typical order from Allison and Jedd, this guide helps operators understand what items are commonly requested, the specifications to consider, and how to process these orders efficiently with suppliers like Toby.

## Standard Signage Items

### A-Frame Signs (Roadside)
**Typical quantity:** 3 units

A-frame signs are positioned roadside to direct traffic to the venue. Standard specifications include:
- Directional arrows pointing toward venue
- QR codes for digital menu access or booking information
- Weather-resistant materials suitable for outdoor placement
- Reflective or high-visibility design for safety

**Common placement:** Positioned at key decision points along approach roads where customers might miss the turning.

### Flags
**Typical quantity:** 4 units

Flags provide visual landmarks and brand visibility from distance. Ensure supplier confirms:
- Mounting mechanism (ground spike, base plate, or pole-mounted)
- Size and material durability for all-weather use
- Branding/artwork requirements

### Lane Bottom Sign
**Specification:** Road sign style

This sign marks the entrance to the venue lane. The "road sign" style typically means:
- Similar construction to highway signage (aluminum composite or rigid substrate)
- Clear visibility from vehicle approach
- Professional appearance consistent with wayfinding standards

### Boundary Line Sign
**Typical quantity:** 1 unit

Marks the property boundary. Confirm with supplier:
- Mounting method (post-mounted or freestanding)
- Required messaging (private property, venue boundary, etc.)
- Size appropriate to visibility requirements

### Walk Fence Signs (Tall)
**Typical quantity:** 2 units

These are positioned along walking routes or fence lines where customers approach on foot. "Tall" specification suggests:
- Elevated height for visibility over terrain or vegetation
- Ground-spike or post-mounted installation
- Directional or informational content

## Menu Ordering

### Size Specification
**Standard size:** A5 (148mm × 210mm)

A5 is half the size of A4 and is the most common compact menu size. Always confirm with the client if they've specified "smaller than A4" to ensure A5 is correct.

### Material Quality
**Recommended:** Thin card stock (minimum 250gsm)

Avoid standard paper for menus as it:
- Feels cheap and damages brand perception
- Tears easily with handling
- Doesn't withstand moisture or spills

**Better alternatives:**
- 300gsm coated card stock (good balance of quality and cost)
- 350-400gsm uncoated card (premium feel)
- Laminated card for high-durability environments

### Quantity Considerations
The original email requests "some more menus" without specifying quantity. Always clarify:
- How many menus are currently in circulation
- Expected replacement rate
- Whether this is a reprint (same design) or new artwork

## Processing the Order

### Step 1: Confirm Specifications
Before requesting pricing, verify with the client:
- Exact quantities for each item type
- Preferred materials and finishes
- Timeline requirements
- Budget parameters

### Step 2: Artwork Requirements
Check if supplier needs new artwork or can reuse previous designs:
- QR codes may need updating if linking to new content
- Menu content likely requires updated artwork
- Signage may use existing brand templates

### Step 3: Request Supplier Quote
Provide supplier with complete specifications including:
- Item descriptions and quantities
- Material preferences
- Installation requirements (if supplier is providing)
- Delivery location and timeline

### Step 4: Client Approval
Submit quote to client with:
- Itemized pricing breakdown
- Lead time for production
- Any artwork deadlines
- Installation costs (if applicable)

### Step 5: Confirm and Order
Once approved:
- Submit purchase order to supplier
- Confirm artwork delivery deadline
- Schedule delivery or installation
- Confirm payment terms

## Common Pitfalls

> **WARNING:** Always confirm QR code destinations before printing. QR codes linking to outdated menus, broken URLs, or incorrect booking systems are expensive mistakes.

> **NOTE:** Outdoor signage requires planning permission in some locations. Verify local authority requirements before ordering permanent installations.

> **TIP:** Order 10-20% extra menus beyond immediate needs. Small reprints are disproportionately expensive compared to slightly larger initial orders.
