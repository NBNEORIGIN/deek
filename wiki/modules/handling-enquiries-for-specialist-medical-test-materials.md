# Handling Enquiries for Specialist Medical Test Materials

This article covers how to respond to print enquiries involving copyrighted or clinically-regulated medical testing materials, specifically Ishihara colour vision test plates. It documents the commercial, legal, and quality assurance issues that prevent us from reproducing these items, and provides guidance on extracting specifications for alternative quotes while maintaining customer relationships.

## Summary

When customers request printing of medical test materials like Ishihara plates, we cannot fulfil these orders due to copyright restrictions and clinical accuracy requirements. The correct approach is to clearly explain why we cannot supply these items, offer to quote alternative components of the order, and gather missing specifications for standard products we can deliver. This maintains trust and usually results in a viable alternative quote.

## Why We Cannot Print Ishihara Cards

**Copyright**: Ishihara colour vision test plates are copyrighted by Kanehara Trading Inc. (Japan). Reproducing them requires a valid reproduction licence, which customers rarely possess and we cannot verify.

**Clinical accuracy**: Even if copyright were not an issue, Ishihara plates only function as valid diagnostic tools when printed to ISO-standardised colour specifications on approved paper stock. Standard digital printing cannot guarantee these tolerances. Supplying plates that *look* correct but don't *function* correctly exposes us to liability and damages the customer's testing programme.

**⚠️ Warning**: Never agree to "just print what the customer sends" for medical test materials. If something goes wrong clinically, "the customer supplied the artwork" is not adequate protection.

## Standard Response Framework

When declining medical test printing, use this structure:

1. **Acknowledge the specific item** (e.g., "On the Ishihara cards specifically...")
2. **Explain copyright position** (licence required, not sighted)
3. **Explain clinical accuracy issue** (controlled printing standards required)
4. **Frame positively** ("we'd rather flag that upfront than supply something that doesn't function as one")
5. **Pivot to what we CAN quote** ("We're happy to quote everything else...")

This maintains professionalism while protecting both parties.

## Extracting Missing Specifications

The initial enquiry typically lacks detail. Use the response to gather:

### Size Clarifications

When customers request non-standard sizes (e.g., "A3 roller banners"):
- Identify the likely intent (desktop pull-up vs. mounted print)
- Offer specific alternatives with dimensions in millimetres
- In this case: "400×600mm desktop pull-up banners" or "A3 prints on display stand"

### Artwork Requirements

Always confirm:
- **Format**: "Print-ready PDFs at correct size"
- **Bleed**: "with 3mm bleed" (many customers don't know what this means—be prepared to explain)
- **Supplied vs. created**: Establish whether we're printing supplied files or designing from scratch

If customer asks "what is 3mm bleed?", explain briefly: extra margin beyond trim edge to prevent white borders if cutting is slightly off.

### Logistics

Extract:
- **Delivery address** (full postcode, company name, specific building if applicable)
- **Collection preference** (from Alnwick unit)
- **Deadline** (specific date or timeframe like "3-5 days from order date")
- **Contact number** (for courier)

### Payment Terms

State clearly:
- "Payment in full on order acceptance"
- "Quote valid 14 days"

## Revising Quantities Based on Clarifications

When customer removes excluded items (like Ishihara cards), confirm revised quantities:

**Original request**: 156 A5 cards (thank you + Ishihara)  
**Revised request**: 110 cards (thank you only)

Always restate the updated quantity in your quote to avoid confusion.

## Processing the Clarified Enquiry

Once specifications are confirmed:

1. Log the enquiry in Cairn with all gathered details
2. Generate Quote 2 (without Ishihara) as requested
3. Include:
   - 2× A1 5mm foamex banners
   - 2× 800mm × 2000mm roller banners
   - 2× desktop pull-up banners (400×600mm, confirm stand type)
   - 110× A5 cards, 250-300gsm silk/uncoated (confirm finish preference)
4. Price delivery to Gosforth postcode
5. Note 3-5 day turnaround requirement (flag if not achievable)
6. Attach standard terms document

## Common Pitfalls

- **Assuming A3 means A3**: "A3 roller banner" is not a standard product—always clarify
- **Ignoring the "why"**: Customers often don't understand why we can't print medical materials—explain fully to avoid frustration
- **Printing without bleed confirmation**: Causes trim issues and reprints
- **Not offering alternatives**: Customer may abandon enquiry entirely if we just say "no" to one item
