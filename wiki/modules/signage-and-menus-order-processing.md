# Signage and Menus Order Processing

This article covers the standard process for handling signage and menu orders for NBNE venues, including roadside signage with QR codes, flags, boundary markers, and printed menu materials. These requests typically come from venue operators looking to improve wayfinding and customer experience at their sites.

## Order Components Breakdown

When processing a signage and menu order, systematically categorize items into distinct groups:

### 1. Wayfinding Signage

**A-Frame Signs (Roadside)**
- Typically ordered in sets of 3 for roadside positioning
- Must include directional arrows
- Require QR code integration for digital menus or booking systems
- Position strategically along access routes to guide customers

**Lane Entry Sign**
- Described as "road sign style" - robust, professional appearance
- Usually mounted at the bottom of access lanes
- Should meet visibility standards for approaching vehicles

### 2. Boundary and Perimeter Markers

**Boundary Line Sign**
- Single unit marking property boundaries
- Important for customer awareness and liability purposes
- Coordinate placement with venue layout

**Walk Fence Signs (Tall Format)**
- Standard order: 2 units
- Mounted on perimeter fencing
- Height is key specification - ensure "tall" dimensions are confirmed with supplier

### 3. Flags

- Standard order quantity: 4 units
- Confirm branding specifications (NBNE logo, venue-specific branding)
- Include mounting hardware in quote

### 4. Menu Printing

**Standard Specifications:**
- Size: A5 (148mm × 210mm) - confirm this is half of A4
- Material: Card stock or similar durable material (NOT standard paper)
- Quality tier: Mid-range - must withstand handling but remain cost-effective
- Quantity: Always confirm required print run

## Processing Steps

1. **Acknowledge Receipt** - Confirm order details with venue operator within 24 hours
2. **Artwork Audit** - Check existing artwork library for all signage types; identify any gaps requiring new design work
3. **Supplier Quote** - Request itemized pricing from approved signage supplier(s)
4. **Material Confirmation** - For menus, provide 2-3 card stock options with samples if possible
5. **Quote Presentation** - Send detailed breakdown to venue operator for approval
6. **Production Timeline** - Communicate expected delivery dates (typical lead time: 10-15 working days)
7. **Installation Coordination** - Determine if installation is included or requires separate arrangement

## Artwork Requirements Checklist

Before finalizing any order, verify:

- [ ] Current NBNE branding guidelines are applied
- [ ] QR codes are generated and tested (must link to correct destination)
- [ ] Directional arrows are clear and unambiguous
- [ ] Text is legible at intended viewing distances
- [ ] Venue-specific information is accurate
- [ ] Weather-resistant specifications are met for outdoor signage

## Common Pitfalls

⚠️ **QR Code Testing**: Always test QR codes before printing. A non-functional QR code on permanent signage is costly to rectify.

⚠️ **Menu Material Quality**: "Thin card" can mean different things to different suppliers. Specify GSM weight (recommend 250-300 GSM for durability without excessive cost).

⚠️ **Planning Permission**: Roadside signage may require local authority approval. Check venue-specific restrictions before ordering.

⚠️ **Sign Placement**: Confirm exact positioning before production, especially for boundary markers. Moving installed signs wastes time and budget.

⚠️ **Flag Mounting**: Verify mounting points exist or include poles/brackets in the order. Flags without mounts create installation delays.

## Pricing Considerations

- A-frames: Mid-range cost; QR code integration adds minimal expense
- Flags: Variable cost depending on size and print method
- Road-style signs: Higher cost due to durability requirements
- Menu printing: Price scales with quantity; recommend minimum order of 100-200 for cost efficiency
- Installation: May double overall project cost if specialist mounting required

## Best Practices

- Consolidate orders where possible to reduce supplier setup fees
- Keep digital records of all artwork for future reorders
- Photograph installed signage for reference library
- Order 10% extra menus as buffer stock
- Schedule signage maintenance checks quarterly
