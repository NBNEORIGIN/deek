# Marketing List Procurement Requests

## Summary

Selectabase Ltd is a data broker offering targeted mailing lists for B2B marketing campaigns. They periodically contact NBNE requesting information about our target audience to provide quotes on data records, contact methods, and costs. These unsolicited emails are part of their sales prospecting and should be handled according to our standard marketing vendor evaluation process—do not respond unless there is an active, approved marketing initiative requiring purchased contact data.

## Email Characteristics

**Sender:** James Flashman, Managing Director, Selectabase Ltd  
**Company Location:** The Archive Centre, Honeywood Road, Dover  
**Contact:** 01304 383838  
**Typical Subject Lines:** "Tell me your target audience" or similar audience-focused queries

### Common Email Features
- Requests definition of "ideal target audience"
- Promises immediate turnaround with data record counts, outreach methods, and pricing
- Often references timing ("new financial year," "get ahead of competitors")
- Includes footer promoting local services (e.g., "Targeted mailing lists in Alnwick," "TPS clean B2B lists")
- Standard legal disclaimers and GDPR compliance statements

## Recommended Response

**Default Action: No Response Required**

Unless you have a specific, pre-approved marketing campaign requiring purchased contact lists, these emails should be filed or deleted without response.

### When to Consider Engagement

Only engage with list brokers if ALL of the following conditions are met:

1. **Budget Approval**: Marketing expenditure has been approved by management
2. **Campaign Planning**: A specific campaign is in active planning stages
3. **Data Requirement**: Internal contact data is insufficient for campaign goals
4. **Legal Review**: Compliance with GDPR and data protection requirements has been verified
5. **Vendor Evaluation**: Multiple vendors are being compared through proper procurement process

## ⚠️ Warnings and Common Pitfalls

### Data Protection Risks
- **GDPR Compliance**: Purchased lists must meet strict consent and legitimate interest requirements
- **TPS/CTPS Checking**: Even "TPS clean" lists require verification; regulatory violations carry significant fines
- **Data Quality**: List brokers may provide outdated or inaccurate data; verify update frequency and validation methods

### Business Risks
- **Cost Inefficiency**: Purchased lists typically have lower conversion rates than organic or warm contacts
- **Brand Reputation**: Unsolicited contact from purchased lists can damage NBNE's reputation
- **Competitive Intelligence**: Defining your target audience to external vendors reveals strategic information

### Sales Pressure Tactics
- Urgency language ("new financial year," "get ahead") is designed to prompt quick decisions
- Request for target audience definition is a sales qualification technique
- "Coming straight back" with information creates obligation to continue conversation

## Alternative Marketing Approaches

Before purchasing contact lists, consider these more effective strategies:

1. **Organic Lead Generation**: Website optimization, content marketing, SEO
2. **Existing Customer Database**: Clean and segment current customer data
3. **Referral Programs**: Leverage satisfied customers for introductions
4. **Industry Partnerships**: Co-marketing with complementary businesses
5. **LinkedIn/Social Selling**: Direct engagement with decision-makers
6. **Local Networking**: Chamber of Commerce, business groups, trade associations

## Vendor Evaluation (If Proceeding)

Should management approve list procurement, evaluate Selectabase and competitors on:

- **Data Source Transparency**: How they collect and update records
- **Consent Documentation**: Proof of GDPR-compliant consent or legitimate interest
- **Targeting Granularity**: Ability to filter by industry, company size, role, geography
- **Data Freshness**: Update frequency and validation methods
- **Pricing Structure**: Per-record costs, minimum purchases, licensing terms
- **Performance Guarantees**: Deliverability rates, bounce-back policies, refund terms
- **References**: Case studies from similar print/sign businesses
