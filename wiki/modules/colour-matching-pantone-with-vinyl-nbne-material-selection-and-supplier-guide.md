# Colour Matching Pantone with Vinyl — NBNE Material Selection and Supplier Guide

## Overview

When customers request specific Pantone colour matches for vinyl graphics, NBNE faces the choice between pre-coloured cast/calendered vinyl or printed alternatives. This article outlines NBNE's approach to vinyl colour matching based on internal communications and supplier interactions.

## Pre-Coloured Vinyl Approach

### Standard Vehicle Graphics Vinyl

For vehicle decals and general applications where close (but not exact) colour matching is acceptable, NBNE requests standard cast vinyl from **FFIS** (primary vinyl supplier).

**Example specification from April 2026:**
- Material: Metamark MD5 or similar
- Width: 610mm
- Length: As required (5m typical minimum)
- Application: Vehicle decals
- Contact: Lyndsey Gray (lgray@ffis.co.uk)

NBNE refers to colours descriptively rather than by Pantone number when ordering stock vinyl (e.g., "post box red"). FFIS typically suggests **Image Perfect** brand as an alternative when confirming specifications.

### Limitations

Pre-coloured vinyl provides limited Pantone accuracy. Colours are restricted to manufacturer's standard ranges, which may not match specific Pantone requirements exactly. This approach is cost-effective for:
- Vehicle graphics
- Non-critical colour applications
- Simple cut vinyl lettering

## Printed Vinyl for Exact Colour Matching

### UV Roll-to-Roll Printing

For precise Pantone matching, NBNE uses **UV roll-to-roll printing** to produce custom-coloured vinyl. This method allows accurate colour reproduction to match Pantone specifications.

**November 2024 application example:**
- Project: Large format wall graphics (~5m x 1.2m)
- Method: UV printer tiling into vertical wallpaper-style strips
- Substrate: ACM panels providing flat mounting surface
- Installation consideration: Graphics alignment critical across tiles

### Material Considerations for Large Format

When using UV-printed vinyl for large installations, material selection affects installation ease:

**NBNE enquiry to FFIS (November 2024):**
- Recommended: Air release vinyl
- Installation aid: Application spray
- Substrate: ACM sheets mounted to wall
- Timeline pressure: Installation required before following Monday (typical rush scenario)

**Contact for technical vinyl advice:** Amanda Vasey (avasey@ffis.co.uk)

## UV Printing vs Pre-Coloured: Decision Factors

### When to Use UV Printed Vinyl

- Exact Pantone colour match required
- Multi-colour gradients or photographic elements
- Matching existing branded colours (e.g., brown lettering referenced in February 2026 salon sign project)
- Complex graphics requiring precise alignment

**February 2026 salon sign case study:**
Customer requested adding salon number to existing sign with brown 3D lettering. NBNE offered two options:

1. **Brown acrylic** (sourced from Belgium — longer lead time)
2. **UV printed and laminated vinyl** (faster turnaround)

This demonstrates UV printing as a viable alternative when specialty materials face supply delays.

### When to Use Pre-Coloured Vinyl

- Standard colours acceptable
- Simple cut graphics
- Vehicle wraps and decals
- Cost-sensitive projects
- Quick turnaround with local stock availability

### Recommended Practice

When customers request Pantone colour matching:

1. **Clarify accuracy requirements:** Is close match acceptable or exact match essential?
2. **Check timeline:** UV printing offers flexibility but requires production time; pre-coloured vinyl may be faster if suitable colour available
3. **Consider substrate:** Some applications (vehicle graphics) have standard material requirements
4. **Factor lead times:** Non-standard colours may require extended procurement
