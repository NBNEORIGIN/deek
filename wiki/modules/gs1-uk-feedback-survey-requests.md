# GS1 UK Feedback Survey Requests

## Summary

GS1 UK periodically sends feedback survey requests to NBNE via email, typically offering prize draw incentives (e.g., £250 John Lewis vouchers) for participation. These surveys are commissioned through third-party research agencies like YouGov to gather member feedback on GS1 UK's services, platforms (My GS1, My Numberbank), and support offerings. While these emails appear legitimate, NBNE operators should follow standard procedures for handling external survey requests and understand when participation is appropriate versus when to disregard.

## Email Characteristics

These emails typically exhibit the following features:

- **Sender**: GS1 UK official communications domain (comms.gs1uk.org)
- **Signatory**: Senior leadership (CEO Anne Godfrey or similar)
- **Incentive**: Prize draw entry for vouchers/gift cards
- **Third-party involvement**: Independent research agency (e.g., YouGov)
- **Deadline**: Specific date for survey completion
- **Multiple CTAs**: Repeated "complete survey" links throughout email
- **Branding**: Official GS1 UK logos and formatting

## Standard Operating Procedure

### 1. Initial Assessment

When receiving a GS1 UK survey request:

- **Verify sender authenticity** via email headers and domain
- **Check with line management** before clicking any links
- **Confirm whether NBNE has an active GS1 UK membership** that would warrant this communication
- **Assess timing** – determine if this aligns with known GS1 UK campaign schedules

### 2. Link Verification

**⚠️ WARNING**: Never click survey links without verification, even from apparently legitimate sources.

- All links should redirect to official GS1 UK domains
- Use link preview tools if available before clicking
- Report suspicious link patterns to IT Security
- Consider whether the email was expected or unsolicited

### 3. Decision Matrix

**Participate if**:
- NBNE holds active GS1 UK membership relevant to your role
- Line management approves participation
- The feedback relates to services NBNE actively uses
- You have been specifically delegated to provide organizational feedback

**Do not participate if**:
- You are unsure of NBNE's GS1 UK membership status
- The request seems generic/mass-distributed to irrelevant contacts
- Links fail verification checks
- You haven't received management approval

### 4. Response Handling

If choosing to participate:

- Complete surveys only during authorized work time
- Provide feedback that reflects NBNE's organizational position, not personal views
- Avoid disclosing sensitive internal NBNE information
- Document that you completed the survey and when (for reference)

If choosing not to participate:

- Use the "Unsubscribe" link at the bottom if communications are irrelevant
- Alternatively, use "Manage preferences" to adjust communication settings
- File/archive the email appropriately in your email management system

## Common Pitfalls

### Phishing Risk

**⚠️ CAUTION**: Survey request emails are common phishing vectors.

- GS1 UK branding can be spoofed
- Prize draw incentives are classic social engineering tactics
- Multiple hyperlinks increase attack surface
- "Final chance" language creates false urgency

Always verify authenticity before engaging.

### Data Privacy Considerations

- Third-party research agencies (like YouGov) will collect your responses
- Review their privacy policy before providing information
- Consider what organizational data you're sharing
- Check NBNE's data sharing policies regarding external surveys

### Time Management

- Survey completion may take 10-20 minutes
- Deadline pressure ("final chance") shouldn't override proper authorization
- Don't allow prize draws to influence business decisions

### Unauthorized Commitment

- Providing feedback may create implied expectations from GS1 UK
- Don't commit NBNE to service changes or upgrades without authority
- Feedback should reflect current usage, not aspirational use cases

## Contact Points

If you need clarification:

- **For GS1 UK queries**: marketing@gs1uk.org (as stated in email)
- **Internal escalation**: Contact your line manager or the procurement team
- **IT Security concerns**: Report to NBNE IT Security team immediately
